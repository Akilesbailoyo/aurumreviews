## Cultural Adaptation Rules
### Finance Content
- Spain: Include Banco de España, CNMV regulatory context
- Germany: Bafin, extreme risk-aversion — lead with safety, not returns
- USA: SEC/FINRA aware, include "not financial advice" prominently
- UK: FCA regulated products preferred

### Parenting Content  
- USA: Lead with safety certifications (JPMA, ASTM)
- Germany: TÜV and GS marks are trust signals
- Spain/Italy: Extended family involvement is cultural norm — address grandparent buyers
- Brazil: Price sensitivity high — always include budget option

## What NOT to Translate Literally
- Humor (always rewrite for local sensibility)
- Idiomatic phrases ("best bang for your buck" → localize concept, not words)
- Product recommendations (a US product may not ship to/be available in Spain)
- Prices (always convert AND verify local availability)
- Affiliate links (must use country-specific program links)

## Output Format for Translations
Always return:
1. Translated text (culturally adapted)
2. Products replaced/added for local market
3. Affiliate programs recommended for this country
4. Any regulatory notes for this market
